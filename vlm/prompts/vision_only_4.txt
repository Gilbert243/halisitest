HAIR TYPE INTERPRETATION (MASK-ADAPTED)

Straight
1A:
• Very smooth, narrow, elongated silhouette
• Minimal boundary irregularity
• Low apparent volume and spread

1B:
• Mostly smooth silhouette with slight outward variation
• Moderate width or body
• Minor boundary undulations

1C:
• Straight-dominant shape but visibly thicker silhouette
• Coarser outline, mild irregularity
• Higher apparent volume than 1A/1B

Wavy
2A:
• Gently undulating outline
• Slight S-like expansion at the silhouette level
• Mostly elongated shape

2B:
• Repeated outward-inward variations along the boundary
• Moderate silhouette complexity
• Balanced spread and density

2C:
• Strong silhouette expansion and irregular boundary
• High apparent volume
• Dense mask with pronounced outline variation
• NO clear evidence of compact looping structures

Curly
3A:
• Rounded or clustered silhouette regions
• Moderate-to-high density
• Consistent curved boundary patterns at a global level

3B:
• Compact, dense silhouette
• Reduced elongation
• High boundary complexity and inward folding

3C:
• Very dense, tightly packed silhouette
• Minimal empty space within the mask
• Highly irregular, compact outline

Coily
4A:
• Extremely dense silhouette
• Strong inward compactness
• Small overall spread relative to density

4B:
• Dense, block-like or angular silhouette
• Jagged or zig-zag boundary characteristics

4C:
• Maximum density and compactness
• Minimal distinguishable outline structure
• Nearly solid, clustered mask appearance

CRITICAL DECISION RULES (MASK-ONLY)

1. Proxy Geometry Rule (MOST IMPORTANT)
True spiral loops are NOT visible in a mask.
Use silhouette compactness, density, and boundary complexity as proxies.

2. Dominant Structure Rule
Classify based on the dominant morphological structure
covering ≥60% of the visible mask area.

3. Density & Compactness Progression
• Lower density, elongated silhouettes → lower types (1–2)
• Higher density, compact silhouettes → higher types (3–4)

4. Boundary Complexity Progression
• Smooth boundary → straighter types
• Highly irregular or jagged boundary → curlier/coily types

5. Uncertainty Rule
If the mask does not provide sufficient morphological evidence
to confidently assign a type, return "Unknown".
